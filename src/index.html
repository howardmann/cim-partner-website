Why it's hard to cross alone

Firms that try to cross alone get stuck.

Building the platform a modern MBCx contract demands is a multi-year software job. It requires, minimum:

  1. 01 A normalised data layer across every BMS protocol in your portfolio.
  2. 02 A tagging and modelling stack that doesn't need hand-assembly on every site.
  3. 03 A fault detection library battle-tested across thousands of assets.
  4. 04 An alert-to-action workflow your clients will actually live inside.
  5. 05 An AI investment budget that keeps compounding while your competitors catch up.

$40M+ invested. 10+ years of R&D and engineering algorithms. Battle-tested across hundreds of millions of square feet globally and thousands of end users taking action. That's the gap any firm building alone has to close.

A tagging framework isn't a platform. A rules editor isn't a delivered capability. Neither is a data layer on its own. Services firms need managed capability their team walks into on day one.

A continuous assurance layer, sold as a recurring service on top of your existing dispatch business.

A mid-rise office portfolio requires 1,000+ verification hours per building per year under recognised maintenance standards. Most dispatch teams can physically deliver less than a third of that. The result is a 72% assurance gap your clients are carrying whether they name it or not.

PEAK closes 52% of those required checks automatically, running 24×7 across hundreds of thousands of checks per portfolio per year. No technicians replaced. The hours you were already going to dispatch now land on the other 48%: corrective action, diagnosis, and repair that only humans can do.
